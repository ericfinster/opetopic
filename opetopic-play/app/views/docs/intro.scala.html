Introduction

This site is dedicated to a certain approach to the theory of higher categories based on a collection of shapes called the opetopes. Among the approaches know to the theory of higher categories, it is perhaps one of the least well known, and it is fair to say that it is not in common usage today.

Part of the reason for this, is that a rigorous definition of opetopic higher category, at least in the sense of a definition given in the language of sets, is both a non-trivial undertaking, and at first sight, probably extremely counterintuitive.

The theory of higher categories is playing a more and more crucial role in modern mathematics and physics. The cobordism hypothesis and its relationship to the study of Topological Quantum Field Theories is a great indication of the importance of higher categories in physics. In algebraic geometry and homotopy theory, the study of derived algebraic geometry seems to find its nautral home in the theory of higher categories. And in logic, Univalent Type Theory is revealing the natural structue of constructive equality as a purely higher categorical phenomenon.

By now, there are many reasonable definitions of the notion of weak higher category. One could say that what is missing currently is not any theoretical gap in understanding, which of course is not to say that we understand all there is about higher category theory. Rather, I would argue, perhaps one of the most important problems facing the theory of higher categories is best regarded as an engineering problem. That is, we have many plausible answers to the question "What is a higher category?". Arguably the more important question today is "How do I work in a higher category?"

Why Opetopes?

There are many other approaches to higher category theory, what makes the opetopes a good choice for this kind of project?

From a constructive, logical point of view, the opetopes are unique in that they can be viewed as a natural diagrammatic way of reasoning which works in all dimensions.

One of the main difficulties facing the theory of higher categories is the fact that setting up the theory with existing foundational systems is an emmense task. Quite a bit of technical machinery is needed in order to prove even the most elementary assertions. The goal of this project is to explore the idea that having a direct syntax for manipulating higher dimensional pasting diagrams could make for a more natural and elementary approach.

One of the attractive features of ordinary category theory is the theory of commutative diagrams. Category theorists learn early on how to express their ideas using commutative diagrams, and most modern mathematics papers in algebra, topology, category theory, logic and more feature these diagrams. It is probably fair to say that a great deal of modern mathematics is written in a combination of English prose and commutative diagrams.

There have been a number of efforts to set various types of formal systems based on commutative diagrams. (Ref ..) I'll mention just three of them here:

Q-systems
Sketches
Graph based logic

All of these systems build on the idea that commutative diagrams are certain kinds of graphs, and propose various rules for manipulating and making deductions using these graphs.

By contrast, the opetopic approach considers the basic formulas to be constructed from trees. This author feels that this is much more inline with out natural idea about sytax as constructed from tree like objects by certain grammar.
