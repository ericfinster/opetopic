Opetopes

Unlike many other types of shapes for higher category theory, which are often simple, having a single representative in each dimension, there are many, many opetopes. This complicates the task of giving a rigorous set-theoretic definition, and there have been a number of attempts and revisions.

However, as compensation for their complexity, the opetopes have an extremely natural diagrammatic representation. In my opinon, this representation is the most direct way to understand what the opetopes are, and why they are useful.

Consequently, we will approach the opetopes diagrammatically, illustrating how we can build them by some very simple rules. This will be sufficient for all the applications on this site.

The key to understanding opetope diagrams is to understand the following two representations of the same tree:

@docs.svgs.boxtree()

Both diagrams represent the same structure: a rooted tree. However, in each of the two cases, the information about this structure is presented differently. The picture on the right is probably more familiar: the tree is depicted as a graph with nodes and edges.

On the left, we have presented the same tree, but where the tree structure is now described by the nesting of the boxes, one inside another. The pattern is simple: the descendents of a given edge are exactly the boxes it contains.

The power of opetopic diagrams will come from combining these two different representations at the same time to represent higher dimensional informaion.
